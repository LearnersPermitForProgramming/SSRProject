Main Theme:

The Ignorance of the Priveleged:

The most priveleged people of Panem live in the Capitol, and their ignorance stems from the fact that they're insulated from the hardships the people of the districts faced. They are wealthy and always have enough to eat, and unlike the districts, aren't forced into exhausting/dangerous labor. Also unlike the districts, their children don't have to participate in the Hunger Games. Because their lives are comfortable and secure, they don't have to think of what people in the districts think, like how to feed their families. As a result, many Capitol citizens are completely oblivious of the harsh realities that people face in Panem.
