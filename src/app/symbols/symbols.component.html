Symbols:

Mockingjay:

The most prominent symbol in Catching Fire is the mockingjay. In the first book (The Hunger Games), the symbol of the mockinjay is used to represent the friendship between Katniss and Rue, but in Catching Fire, the mockingjay came to symbolize Katniss herself. Katniss herself represents defiance of the Capitol, and further became a symbol of the rebellion. For example, two refugee women from District 8 used the symbol in the woods show Katniss a cracker with the imagery of the mockinjay. Furthermore, the game maker of the 75th annual Hunger Games, Plutarch Heavensbee, uses a mockingjay on his watch's face for the sole purpose of showing he is part of the rebellion.

Bow and Arrows:

The bow and arrows have a very significant meaning to Katniss: Security. These weapons allow her to hunt and symbolizes that her family has survived so long because of the hunting. Katniss believes as long as she has the weapons, she will be able to continue to feed herself/family. In the Hunger Games arena, the bow and arrows represent security in the sense that Katniss is able to protect herself. Therefore, they give her a feeling of confidence/safety in the midst of the uncertainty and hostility in the Hunger Games.
