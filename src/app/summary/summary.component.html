Summary:

The Hunger Games have passed and Katniss' and Peeta's lives have changed substantially. Katniss finds President Snow waiting for her after she has returned home, and threatens her family if she encourages any more public defiance of the Capitol. As part of a plan with Haymitch, she continues to pretend as if she is in love with Peeta and ends up marrying him too. Weeks later, Katniss goes back into the woods and is shocked to find two women, one of which holds out a cracker with the symbol of a mockingjay on it. They explain their journey to the supposedly destroyed District 13, which they believe is now home to underground rebels. At this time, the Capitol announces the 3rd quarter quell, who's tributes will be reaped from the current victors. The tributes are sent to the arena, which was a small tropical island, and the 3rd quarter quell begins. After realizing her allies and a close call with death, Katniss and her newfound allies (Finnick, Mags, and Peeta) head to the jungle, where a toxic fog descends over to them, which causes their legs to seize up. Since Finnick could only carry Peeta or Mags, Mags sacrifices herself by going into the fog, and the remaining three barely make it to the beach. After finding Joahnna Mason along with her allies, they devise a plan to electrocute the remaining opponents. When they go to carry out the plan, Katniss is suddenly cut, and before she knows what happens, Joahnna hits her head, and cuts her arm. Katniss thinks they're about to kill her, so she wraps some wire (that was going to be used to kill the remaining tributes), around one of her arrows, and fires, knocking her unconscious. A rebel hovercraft retrieves her heading for District 13, where they explain the events of the Hunger Games. In the end of the book, the Capitol drops bombs over District 12, but the rebels rescued Katniss' loved ones.
